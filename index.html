Maze made on a base of 256 squares.
Day of the Programmer, an annual observance that celebrates the positive impact that programmers make in our lives and society. This day falls on the 256th day of the year, which is usually September 13th or September 12th in leap years. The number 256 was chosen because it is the highest power of 2 that is less than 365. In computer science and programming, 256 is a significant number, often seen in array sizes and other aspects due to the binary numbering system. On this day, it's common to acknowledge the hard work, dedication, and creativity that programmers bring into their work. It is also a day to appreciate the complexity and thoughtfulness that goes into coding, debugging, and software development as a whole. This day serves as a reminder of how integral programming is in the modern world, fueling everything from websites and apps to scientific research and artificial intelligence.
